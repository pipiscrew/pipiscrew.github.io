You can stream [over 6,500 full-length movies](https://archive.org/details/feature_films) over at archive.org, including [Night of the Living Dead](https://archive.org/details/night_of_the_living_dead) and [The Internet's Own Boy: The Story of Aaron Swartz](https://archive.org/details/TheInternetsOwnBoyTheStoryOfAaronSwartz).

They've even got a [collection of classic cartoons](https://archive.org/details/animationandcartoons), like [Tom and Jerry](https://archive.org/details/126BuddiesThickerThanWater1962), [Betty Boop](https://archive.org/details/BettyBoopCartoons), [the Pink Panther](https://archive.org/details/ThePinkPanther-cartoons), and lots of [Popeye](https://archive.org/details/vintage_cartoons) (including one where [Popeye runs for president against Bluto](https://archive.org/details/Popeye_forPresident).)

And [an archive.org blog post](https://blog.archive.org/2020/03/16/7-things-to-do-if-you-cant-leave-the-house/) explains that that's just the beginning:If gaming is more your speed, then check out the [MS-DOS Games](https://archive.org/details/softwarelibrary_msdos_games) in our Software Library. This collection includes dozens of classic favorites such as [Pac-Man](https://archive.org/details/msdos_Pac-Man_1983), [Sim City](https://archive.org/details/msdos_SimCity_1989), [The Oregon Trail](https://archive.org/details/msdos_Oregon_Trail_The_1990), [Doom](https://archive.org/details/msdos_Doom_-_The_Roguelike_2005), [Prince of Persia](https://archive.org/details/msdos_Prince_of_Persia_1990), [Donkey Kong](https://archive.org/details/msdos_Donkey_Kong_1983), and [Tetris](https://archive.org/details/softwarelibrary_msdos_games?and%5B%5D=tetris&sin=), as well as many more lesser-known titles such as [Aliens Ate My Baby Sitter!](https://archive.org/details/msdos_Commander_Keen_6_-_Aliens_Ate_My_Baby_Sitter_1991) and [Freddy Pharkas, Frontier Pharmacist](https://archive.org/details/msdos_Freddy_Pharkas_Frontier_Pharmacist_1993). Enjoy simulations of popular board and card games such as [Monopoly](https://archive.org/details/msdos_Monopoly_Deluxe_1992) [press F1 to begin], [Stratego](https://archive.org/details/msdos_Stratego_1990), [Hearts](https://archive.org/details/msdos_Hoyle_Classic_Card_Games_1993), or [Mah Jong](https://archive.org/details/msdos_Mah_Jongg_Solitaire_1987), as well as [flight simulators](https://archive.org/details/softwarelibrary_msdos_games?and%5B%5D=flight&sin=), [sports games](https://archive.org/details/softwarelibrary_msdos_games?and%5B%5D=sports&sin=), and this [treat for Monty Python fans](https://archive.org/details/msdos_Monty_Pythons_Flying_Circus_1990).
They also have recordings of [old-time radio shows](https://archive.org/details/oldtimeradio) -- as well as [an archive of live music](https://archive.org/details/etree). ("Our most popular collection by far is [The Grateful Dead](https://archive.org/details/GratefulDead), but you could also explore [Smashing Pumpkins](https://archive.org/details/SmashingPumpkins), [Robert Randolph (and the Family Band)](https://archive.org/details/RobertRandolphandtheFamilyBand), [Disco Biscuits](https://archive.org/details/DiscoBiscuits), [Death Cab for Cutie](https://archive.org/details/DeathCabforCutie), [John Mayer](https://archive.org/details/JohnMayerMusic), or [Grace Potter and the Nocturnals](https://archive.org/details/GracePotterandtheNocturnals)...")

And then there's this:Relive the 80's and 90's (and learn [how to style your scarf](https://archive.org/details/HowToStyleAScarf)) with the [Ephemeral VHS collection](https://archive.org/details/ephemeralvhs), or roam the cosmos with the [NASA Image of the Day gallery](https://archive.org/details/nasaimageofthedaygallery). Learn about the history of advertising with [this collection of retro TV ads](https://archive.org/details/adviews) or [enjoy some psychedelic screensavers](https://archive.org/details/ElectricSheep). No matter how long you're stuck indoors, the Internet Archive will have something new to offer you — so happy hunting!
